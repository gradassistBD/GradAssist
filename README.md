## 3. CORE SERVICES

### 3.1 Research Proposal Guidance
Most proposals fail not because the idea is weak, but because it lacks focus or connection to the target lab's work. GradAssist helps students identify a clear research question, align it with the target professor's publications, and structure the proposal in the format Korean academic reviewers expect.

### 3.2 Academic CV Development
An academic CV for Korean applications is structured differently from a general resume. GradAssist reorganizes and rewrites CVs to lead with research experience, present publications and projects in the correct academic format, and communicate scholarly trajectory — not just a list of activities.

### 3.3 SOP Structuring & Editing
The Statement of Purpose must establish research identity, demonstrate intellectual seriousness, connect background to future goals, and show fit with the Korean institution. GradAssist works through **multiple revision rounds** — not a single-pass edit — until the document genuinely represents the student's best self.

### 3.4 Professor Outreach Emails
Cold emails to Korean PIs follow a specific etiquette. GradAssist helps craft outreach emails that demonstrate real familiarity with the professor's work, reference their recent publications or NRF projects, and follow the tone and structure Korean academics respond to.

### 3.5 Scholarship Documentation Support
Each scholarship (government, professor-funded, industry-backed) has its own documentation language and priorities. GradAssist prepares and aligns every supporting document with the actual selection criteria — not just the checklist on the portal.

### 3.6 MS & PhD Application Strategy
Personalized guidance on program selection, professor identification, application timelines, and profile positioning. Includes insider knowledge of which labs are currently recruiting.

## 4. FUNDING PATHWAYS GRADASSIST COVERS

GradAssist uses plain language for scholarships — avoid jargon-only references. Always describe funding type first, then the program name in brackets.

### 4.1 Korean Government Scholarship (GKS / KGSP)
- Most prestigious fully-funded award for international graduate students
- Covers: full tuition + ₩900K–1M/month stipend + round-trip airfare + settlement allowance + Korean language training (1 year)
- MS: 3 years | PhD: 4 years
- Minimum CGPA: 2.64/4.0 (competitive applicants typically higher)
- Annual deadlines: February–March (embassy track), April (university track)
- Reviewers assess the full profile — SOP quality, research background, field of study, language scores

### 4.2 Professor-Funded / Lab Funding (BK21 FOUR / NRF)
- Most common route to a fully-funded PhD in Korea
- Professor funds tuition waiver + monthly research stipend from lab budget (NRF grants or BK21 FOUR programme)
- Available at: KAIST, SNU, POSTECH, UNIST, Yonsei, and other top universities
- The professor relationship is the deciding factor — cold email strategy is critical here

### 4.3 Brain Pool Research Fellowship (Government & Industry)
- For PhD holders invited to collaborate with Korean R&D institutions
- Salary: ₩5M–₩25M/month
- Duration: 1–3 years
- Managed by National Research Foundation (NRF)
- 2025 deadline: May 30

### 4.4 Brain Pool+ Fellowship (Tenure Track, 2025)
- For top-tier scientists seeking full-time tenure-track positions at Korean national R&D institutions
- 4+6 year tenure track
- Up to ₩600M/year total package
- Approximately 5 spots nationwide — highly competitive
